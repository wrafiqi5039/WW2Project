Europe

By April 1945, the Soviet troops close in on Berlin from the East along side with the Western Allies troops closing on Berlin from the West. This makes war situation for Germany hopeless. Knowing that it is impposible for Germany to win, Adolf Hitler killed himself inside a bunker in Berlin on April 30, 1945. Germany officially suffered to the Allied powers the next day after Hitler's death. The leaders of Soviet Union and the United States and Great Britain discussed what to do with Germany after the surrender. Post-war negotiations took place at two conferences in 1945, one before the official end of the war, and one after. These conferences set the stage for the beginning of the Cold War and of a divided Europe.


Japan

By the summer of 1945, the defeat of Japan was a foregone conclusion. The Japanese navy and air force were destroyed. The Allied naval blockade of Japan and intensive bombing of Japanese cities had left the country and its economy devastated. At the end of June, the Americans captured Okinawa, a Japanese island from which the Allies could launch an invasion of the main Japanese home islands. The invasion of Japan promised to be the bloodiest seaborne attack of all time, conceivably 10 times as costly as the Normandy invasion in terms of Allied casualties. On July 16, a new option became available when the United States secretly detonated the world’s first atomic bomb in the New Mexico desert. Ten days later, the Allies issued the Potsdam Declaration, demanding the “unconditional surrender of all the Japanese armed forces. However Japan refused to surrender so as a result, U.S. President Harry Truman ordered the devastation to proceed, and on August 6, the U.S. B-29 bomber Enola Gay dropped an atomic bomb on the Japanese city of Hiroshima, killing an estimated 80,000 people and fatally wounding thousands more. Three days later, the Americans dropped a plutonium bomb, on the Japanese city of Nagasaki, killing an additional 40,000 people. On August 15, 1945, the Japanese government officially surrender bringing an end to the world most horrific conflict.
